Application of nail tips is suggested when there is little or no free edge

1) Follow Steps 1-3 of 10-Step Quick Prep procedure to prepare nails. (See Page 5).

2) Choose the tip style that is right for your client. Tips are used to extend the nail and produce a desired shape. They are available in several shapes and sizes to accomodate your clients’ needs and desires. Nouveau Nail Professional Nail Tips are available in four styles, including Form Fit, Shallowell, French White and Crystal Clear.

3) Fit all ten nails, making certain that the well of the tip is even with the sidewalls. 4) Hold tip at the free edge, well side up, and run Extra Strength Glue inside well. 5) Carefully “knock” excess glue onto a towel. Holding the tip down at a 45 degree angle, rock it into position. This technique will prevent trapped air between the well of the tip and the natural nail, which causes air bubbles to form.

6) Once tips are adhered, follow Steps 6-10 of Quick Prep procedure. (See Page 5).

Application of nail forms is typically used when there is a free edge, but additional length is desired

1) Follow Steps 1-3 of the 10-Step Quick Prep procedure to prepare nails. (See Page 5). 2) Peel form from backing.

3) Hold each side of the form between your thumb and pointer fnger and ease the form under the free edge and snug to the sidewall.

4) Ensure there is no space between the free edge and the nail form and that the form itself is level with the natural nail.

5) Pinch the form at the free edge to create a smooth C-curve.

6) Once forms are adhered, follow Steps 9-10 of Quick Prep procedure. (See Page 5).
